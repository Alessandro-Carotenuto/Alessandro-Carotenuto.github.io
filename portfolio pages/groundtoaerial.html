Ground-to-Aerial Image Synthesis with VQ-GAN and Transformers

Overview

This project implements a cross-view synthesis pipeline that transforms ground-level street photographs into synthetic overhead aerial imagery. The system utilizes a VQGAN-Transformer architecture to bridge the extreme geometric gap between terrestrial and satellite viewpoints.

$$P(s) = \prod_{i} p(s_i \mid s_{i}, c_{ground})$$

A pretrained VQGAN encodes both views into discrete token sequences, while a GPT Transformer learns to autoregressively generate satellite tokens conditioned on the ground-level input, trained on the CVUSA dataset.

Key Elements

Cross-View Learning

Learning the correspondence between ground-level street photos and overhead aerial imagery.

VQ-GAN

$z_q = \text{argmin}_{e_k} \|E(x) - e_k\|$

Vector Quantized GAN to encode images into discrete codebook tokens.

Autoregressive Generation

$P(s) = \prod_i p(s_i \mid s_{<i}, c_{ground})$

GPT Transformer predicts satellite tokens sequentially, conditioned on the ground-level token sequence.

Polar Format

Satellite targets are stored as polar-projected images, aligning their radial structure with the geometry of ground-level panoramic photos.
